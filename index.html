Abstract

With the widespread deployment of embodied navigation agents in safety-critical contexts, assessing their robustness against physical adversarial examples becomes imperative. However, existing attack methods for embodied navigation often lack practicality due to the complex transformation from digital world to physical world. To tackle this challenge, in this paper, we propose a physically feasible attack method called ``non-contact adversarial patches". Unlike modifying existing objects in the scene, our approach positions patches near objects to divert the navigation agent's visual attention using learnable textures. We enhance patch effectiveness through a multi-view optimization strategy, leveraging informative gradients from multiple viewpoints via a differentiable rendering process. This allows patches to effectively cloak target objects from critical viewing angles. Experiment results on the fundamental embodied navigation task ObjectNav and scene datasets HM3D demonstrate that our non-contact adversarial patches achieve approximately xx\% reduction in navigation success rates, along with robust cross-scenario transferability. More importantly, these patches can be printed into the real world in just 10 minutes, affirming their practicality. For further details and results, please refer our project page at https://non-contact.github.io/.
